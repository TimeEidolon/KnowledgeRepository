### 

If you’re looking to display lumen units in your rendering software, please follow these straightforward steps to adjust your settings.

### Step 1: Access Your Profile Settings

To begin, locate the profile icon situated in the upper left corner of your project design page. This icon typically represents your user account and provides access to various settings relevant to your workspace.

### Step 2: Select the Preferences Button

Once you click on the profile icon, a dropdown menu will appear. From this menu, select the **Preferences** button. This section allows you to customize various aspects of your project environment, ensuring that your settings align with your design needs.

![](https://qhstatic-us-s3.coohom.com/yuntai/image/png/1742392786319/F562386426F249F8EE9D30EB38064E4D.png)

### Step 3: Change Brightness Measurement Unit

In the Preferences window, scroll down until you find the **Renderings** button. Here you’ll be able to modify how brightness is measured within your renderings.

* Look for an option labeled **Brightness Measurement Unit** or something similar.
* Change the setting to **Lumens** (lm) if it isn’t already selected.

This adjustment will enable your renderings to display lighting output in lumens, providing a clearer understanding of light intensity and distribution within your designs.

![](https://qhstatic-us-s3.coohom.com/yuntai/image/png/1742392792400/468F922263D69885871C5A48A22DA591.png)

### Conclusion

By following these simple steps—accessing your profile settings, selecting preferences, and adjusting brightness measurement units—you can easily show lumen units for lighting in your renderings. This enhancement not only aids in achieving more accurate visualizations but also improves communication regarding lighting plans with clients and collaborators. Mastering this aspect of rendering will undoubtedly elevate the quality of your architectural presentations!
