In the floor plan tool, you can use the align multi-floor units tool to align the upper and lower floors, and this article explains in detail how to align the upper and lower floors with the align multi-floor units tool.

⭐Before reading the article, it is recommended to watch the video tutorial first for easy getting started~

🎥 [Video URL](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=\_rDeKhDLlLc)

## Steps with screenshots

Each step below includes the screenshot used in this article.

### Step 1
Entrance In the upper toolbar, select **"Toolkit - Align multi-floor units - Align with upper/Align with lower"**.

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/image/png/1747987950890/A8CEBABD0663ECBAEEE7516DF7A7D2B5.png)

### Step 2
Align Upper and Lower Floors Click to align the upper or lower floors, the canvas will display the floor structure of the corresponding floor, first select a point on the current floor plan, and then select the point on the upper/lower floor to be aligned, then you can move the floor plan to the corresponding position. * **Note: The basemap will be hidden during the alignment process, and will be restored after the function is canceled.** The align upper effect is demonstrated below:

# Align Upper and Lower Floors

Click to align the upper or lower floors, the canvas will display the floor structure of the corresponding floor, first select a point on the current floor plan, and then select the point on the upper/lower floor to be aligned, then you can move the floor plan to the corresponding position.

* **Note: The basemap will be hidden during the alignment process, and will be restored after the function is canceled.**

The align upper effect is demonstrated below:

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/image/gif/1747989072714/69A5DADBCD5E9E49CD0F450660706890.gif)

The align lower effect is demonstrated below:
