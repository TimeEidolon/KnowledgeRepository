In interior design, composition principles help designers create harmonious, balanced, and visually appealing spaces. In rendering, it determines whether a rendering is professional or ordinary. This article will introduce you to five composition techniques to help your renderings go to the next level~

## Steps with screenshots

Each step below includes the screenshot used in this article.

### Step 1
Rule of Thirds Composition **Role**: The rule of thirds divides a frame or space into three equal parts, enhancing visual balance and focus by placing key elements along the dividing lines or their intersections. **Application**: In interior design, key elements such as furniture, artwork, or lighting fixtures can be placed at the intersections of the rule of thirds lines. This avoids placing important elements in the center, creating a more dynamic and layered space. For example, positioning a sofa or coffee table along the rule of thirds lines can result in a more natural layout.

### Step 2
Golden Ratio Composition **Role**: The golden ratio (approximately 1:1.618) is a classic proportional relationship that creates natural and harmonious beauty. **Application**: In interior design, the golden ratio can be used to determine furniture dimensions, space division, or the proportions of decorative elements. For instance, the length-to-width ratio of a room can approximate the golden ratio, or the height and width of furniture can be designed according to this ratio, making the space appear more coordinated and comfortable.

### Step 3
Symmetrical Composition **Role**: Symmetrical composition creates stability, solemnity, and a sense of order through mirrored or balanced layouts. **Application**: In interior design, symmetrical composition is often used in formal spaces such as living rooms, dining rooms, or entryways. For example, arranging sofas, coffee tables, and artwork symmetrically, or placing lighting fixtures and decorations in a balanced manner, can create a dignified and orderly atmosphere.

### Step 4
Golden Triangle Composition **Role**: Golden triangle composition divides a frame or space into triangles, guiding the viewer’s gaze and enhancing visual dynamism. **Application**: In interior design, furniture or decorative elements can be arranged to form triangles, adding layers and flow to the space. For instance, positioning a sofa, coffee table, and floor lamp in a triangular arrangement, or creating a triangular relationship between wall decorations, lighting, and furniture, can guide the viewer’s eye and enhance the three-dimensionality of the space.

### Step 5
Diagonal Composition **Role**: Diagonal composition uses diagonal lines to increase the sense of movement and depth in a space. **Application**: In interior design, diagonal lines can be created through the placement of furniture, rugs, or decorative elements, making the space more dynamic and expansive. For example, placing a sofa or rug diagonally, or using wall decorations or lighting fixtures to form diagonal lines, can break the monotony of the space and add visual interest.
