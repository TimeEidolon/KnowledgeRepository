## Steps with screenshots

Each step below includes the screenshot used in this article.

### Step 1
Select Material&Component in the left panel. ​

![](https://qhstatic-us-s3.coohom.com/yuntai/image/png/1733853252993/F455D09F27978B747280D5238C04C950.png)
<img src="https://qhstatic-us-s3.coohom.com/yuntai/image/png/1733853252993/F455D09F27978B747280D5238C04C950.png" alt="Step 1" style="max-width:100%;height:auto;" />

### Step 2
Click the face you want to edit. ​

### Step 3
Click **Face Editor** in the floating toolbar. ​

![](https://qhstatic-us-s3.coohom.com/yuntai/image/png/1733853255183/540FA909941AC3D00E1D37110FE9C0CD.png)
<img src="https://qhstatic-us-s3.coohom.com/yuntai/image/png/1733853255183/540FA909941AC3D00E1D37110FE9C0CD.png" alt="Step 3" style="max-width:100%;height:auto;" />

### Step 4
Click the face. ​

### Step 5
Choose **Multi-Boundary** in the floating toolbar.

![](https://qhstatic-us-s3.coohom.com/yuntai/image/png/1733853255966/1108290A11BC82404AC884DFBC4E68A2.png)
<img src="https://qhstatic-us-s3.coohom.com/yuntai/image/png/1733853255966/1108290A11BC82404AC884DFBC4E68A2.png" alt="Step 5" style="max-width:100%;height:auto;" />

### Step 6
Hold down Ctrl to select only one boundary.

### Step 7
Edit in the floating toolbar or under **Boundary Settings** in the left panel.

### Step 8
Click **Done** to confirm.
