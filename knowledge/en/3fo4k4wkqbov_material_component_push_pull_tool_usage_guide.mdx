# **FAQ**

**Q1:** Why can't I push or pull some walls?

**A1:** Only architectural surfaces can be modified in **Material & Component**. Structural surfaces cannot be modified.

**Q2:** What are structural surfaces?

**A2:** The walls, ceilings, and floors that are created automatically when you draw a floor plan. All surfaces that appear when you first enter Material& Component are structural surfaces.

**Q3:** What are architectural surfaces？

**A3:** The surfaces that you draw by using the Line (L), Rect (R), and Push/Pull tools.

**Q4:** What is the difference between architectural surface and structural surface?

**A4:** The difference between architectural surfaces and structural surfaces is that you cannot push, pull, elevate, move, or delete structural surfaces. Moreover, you cannot set a structural surface as a loft path. However, architectural surfaces support all these operations. Meanwhile, both architectural and structural surfaces support material application and advanced material editing.

**​Q5:** Can I modify the floor plan or create door and window openings in **Material & Component**?

**A5:** The above operations are not supported in **Material & Component**.
