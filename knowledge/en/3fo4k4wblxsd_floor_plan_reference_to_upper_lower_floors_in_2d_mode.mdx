In the floor plan tool, you can set the upper or lower floors to be displayed in the floor reference, and you can also hide the floor reference, and this article explains in detail how to refer to the upper/lower floor plan in 2D mode.

⭐Before reading the article, it is recommended to watch the video tutorial first for easy getting started~

🎥 [Video URL](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qw7eef5wAHI)

## Steps with screenshots

Each step below includes the screenshot used in this article.

### Step 1
Switch to **2D mode.**

### Step 2
Click the small eye button, select the **upper** or **lower** in the **floor reference**, and the corresponding floor plan will be displayed as a reference.

# Show Floor References

1. Switch to **2D mode.**

2. Click the small eye button, select the **upper** or **lower** in the **floor reference**, and the corresponding floor plan will be displayed as a reference.

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/image/png/1747984078828/A8CEBABD0663ECBAEEE7516DF7A7D2B5.png)![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/image/gif/1747984120556/75078533B28017C56CDAA0F878F9ED21.gif)

# Hide Floor References

1. Switch to **2D mode.**

2. Click the small eye button, select the **invisible** in the **floor reference**, you will hide other floor plans after you choose.

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/image/png/1747985904123/A8CEBABD0663ECBAEEE7516DF7A7D2B5.png)![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/image/gif/1747985958458/1148ED18F63CA09FDC327D34624BE0BD.gif)
