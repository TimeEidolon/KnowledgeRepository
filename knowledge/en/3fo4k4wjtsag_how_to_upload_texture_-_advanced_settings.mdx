### Step 8
**Displacement**: Displacement maps are grayscale textures you map to objects to create true surface relief (elevations and depressions) on an otherwise flat object. With Displacement maps, depressions and elevations become part of the geometry of the object, changing the topology, unlike Bump maps that only create the illusion of surface relief. Ratio: The replacement ratio should not be too large and can be adjusted according to actual needs Grayscale image: It can distort the overall outline of the object; unlike bump maps, which need to be rendered at an angle, after adding displacement maps, multi-angle rendering has bump effects
