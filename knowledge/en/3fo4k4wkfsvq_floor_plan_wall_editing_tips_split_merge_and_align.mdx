When drawing walls, you can use functions such as wall connection, splitting, alignment, etc., to quickly create floor plans and improve drawing efficiency.

⭐Before reading the article, it's recommended to watch the video tutorial first to get started quickly~

🎥 [Video URL](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f4AvlXnERic)

Each step below includes the screenshot used in this article.

### Step 1
Select the wall, click the **One-click Slice** button in the floating window, and you will see a complete wall divided into two sections.

### Step 2
If you want to quickly split all walls of a floor plan, hold the **shift/⇧** key to select all walls, and then click the floating window's **One-click Slice** button. You will see all walls of the floor plan have been split successfully. Select a wall, click **Split** in the floating window. Move the splitting tool onto the wall where a blue dividing line will appear. Click the left mouse button to split, or input the desired splitting distance in the input box and press **Enter** to complete the split. (Note: Endpoints at wall connections cannot be split.)

# Entrance

Entrance 1: In the floor plan tool, hover the mouse over the top **Edit** , then select **Merge/Split/Align**.

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/image/png/1772776171642/A8CEBABD0663ECBAEEE7516DF7A7D2B5.png)

Entrance 2: Select the wall, then click **Split/Merge** in the floating window.

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/yuntai/image/png/1772784955315/2D37C2734C50A6837B78ACB4959B66FF.png)

# Split the Wall

# 

## One-click Slice

1. Select the wall, click the **One-click Slice** button in the floating window, and you will see a complete wall divided into two sections.

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/image/gif/1772778588484/BDD03DBD7B143CFD44CBE3156FF860F8.gif)

2. If you want to quickly split all walls of a floor plan, hold the **shift/⇧** key to select all walls, and then click the floating window's **One-click Slice** button. You will see all walls of the floor plan have been split successfully.

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/yuntai/image/gif/1772784955489/ECAFD36372696E24C659C84F2867671E.gif)

## Split

Select a wall, click **Split** in the floating window. Move the splitting tool onto the wall where a blue dividing line will appear. Click the left mouse button to split, or input the desired splitting distance in the input box and press **Enter** to complete the split. (Note: Endpoints at wall connections cannot be split.)

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/image/gif/1772779683589/4DCE9FB8A8A5511E39215C44E472F432.gif)

## Set Walls to Auto-Split When Intersecting

1. Click your avatar at the top-right corner, select **Preferences**, and in the pop-up panel, click on the **Floor Plan**, then set the **Wall Intersect Split** to **Auto**.Then click **Close** in the top-right corner.

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/yuntai/image/png/1772784955292/2D37C2734C50A6837B78ACB4959B66FF.png)

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/image/png/1772778899616/A8CEBABD0663ECBAEEE7516DF7A7D2B5.png)

2. Once set, redraw walls on the canvas. You will see all intersecting walls are automatically split, but pre-existing walls are unaffected.

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/yuntai/image/gif/1772784955967/DC6B4BF443B4C99EDE0159C322F833CA.gif)

# Merge the Wall

# 

Walls with equal width at both ends and aligned on the same horizontal line can be connected by **Double Click to Connect Wall**. (Note: Walls with inconsistent thickness, height, or bearing types cannot be connected.)

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/image/gif/1772784549131/4CBF596BEBBAC672B982C639AE197B31.gif)

* You can also select a wall, click the **Merge** button in the floating window, then **Select Neighbor Object**, and it will connect automatically.

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/yuntai/image/gif/1772784955465/BA8EB850DC467FD6A7E311411FC192C3.gif)

# Align the Wall

# 

1. Select a wall and directly move it to the target position.

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/image/gif/1772780430743/97AA0A57E759FF244F7B2BADFDEACA78.gif)

2. Click **Edit** in the top toolbar, select **Align**, then **Select the** **Line that Needs to Aligned**, and **Select the Aligned Line**. You will see both walls aligned perfectly.

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/yuntai/image/gif/1772784955427/7072F965510F52907027876F298619A7.gif)
