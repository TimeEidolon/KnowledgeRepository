Say goodbye to the hassle of tedious adjustments one by one! By cleverly utilizing the group and ungroup functions, you can combine multiple modeling surfaces into a single group with one click, easily achieving unified movement, rotation, and scaling, doubling your design efficiency instantly!


## Steps with screenshots

Each step below includes the screenshot used in this article.

### Step 1
**Entrance** Select **Advanced Tool** and click to enter **Material & Component.**

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/image/png/1779358224054/A8CEBABD0663ECBAEEE7516DF7A7D2B5.png)

### Step 2
Group Method 1: Hold down the **Shift** key and click on multiple modeling surfaces, then click **Group** in the floating window.

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/yuntai/image/gif/1779445205715/4C78CF0CF203DE2E292610643D8D6593.gif)
<img src="https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/yuntai/image/gif/1779445205715/4C78CF0CF203DE2E292610643D8D6593.gif" alt="Step 2" style="max-width:100%;height:auto;" />

### Step 3
Method 2: Hold down the **Shift** key, drag a box from top to bottom to select all the models within the box, and then click **Group.**

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/image/gif/1779359276013/E73BA41856B956C0AF264EC48E2E5F72.gif)

### Step 4
Ungroup Select an already grouped model and click **Ungroup** in the floating window.

# Group

Method 1: Hold down the **Shift** key and click on multiple modeling surfaces, then click **Group** in the floating window.

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/yuntai/image/gif/1779445205715/4C78CF0CF203DE2E292610643D8D6593.gif)

Method 2: Hold down the **Shift** key, drag a box from top to bottom to select all the models within the box, and then click **Group.**

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/image/gif/1779359276013/E73BA41856B956C0AF264EC48E2E5F72.gif)

* Grouped models can be manipulated as a whole for unified movement, rotation, and scaling, greatly improving design efficiency.

# Ungroup
