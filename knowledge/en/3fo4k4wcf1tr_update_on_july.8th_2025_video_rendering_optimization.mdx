# Optimized Improvements

**1. Engine Upgrade**

Video rendering has been upgraded. When using lighting from Fast & Interior Series 3.0 and above, fast rendering now supports more types of dynamic models and newly added materials, including translucent, overlay, and blended materials.

**![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/yuntai/image/jpeg/1751872058819/0BB7DA08FED6DC84C3E8D24BB4EAFCD1.gif)**

**2. Consistent Effects between Video and Image**

The video rendering method adapts to the selected lighting template, with resolution adjusting automatically to ensure the video lighting can be consistent with that of still images.

**![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/yuntai/image/jpeg/1751872058831/6D18299F18874012AD6483269D5F4948.gif)**

# Potential New Questions

**For users of [Ultra-Fast & Realistic v3.0 and above] lighting templates:**

**Q1:** When I'm using the **[Ultra-Fast & Realistic v3.0 and above]** lighting template, and I noticed that **[HQ720P & HQ1080P]** are no longer available for rendering. But I think the video quality was better with those—what should I do?

**A1:** If you prefer the video quality of the previous versions, we recommend using the **[Realistic & Equilibrium]** lighting templates. The rendered videos will match the visual quality you're used to.

**For users of [Realistic & Equilibrium] lighting templates:**

**Q1:** When I used the **[Realistic & Equilibrium]** lighting templates, but I can no longer render 720P or 1080P videos on the same day. What should I do?

**A1:** The parameter settings of these lighting templates do require longer rendering times, so videos can only be delivered overnight. If you wish to receive the video on the same day, we recommend switching to **[Ultra-Fast & Realistic v3.0 and above]** lighting templates.

**Q2:** When I'm rendering a 480P video using the **[Realistic & Equilibrium]** lighting template, and the video seems choppy. Is this a bug?

**A2:** 480P is used as a preview tier. To ensure videos are rendered within the same day, it's rendered at a low frame rate. However, when upgraded to higher resolutions, normal frame rates will be guaranteed.
