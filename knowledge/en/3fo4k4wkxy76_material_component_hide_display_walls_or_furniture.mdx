To help you achieve a clearer editing perspective at different stages of design, you can use the hide/display functionality for walls and furniture to focus more easily on hard furnishing structures or soft furnishing effects.

# For Users

All Users

# Entrance

In the design tool, select **Advanced Tool** and click to enter **Material & Component**.

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/image/png/1779689098853/A8CEBABD0663ECBAEEE7516DF7A7D2B5.png)

# Hide/Display Walls or Furniture

1. In the **Material & Component** tool, click the hide/display icon on the top toolbar to switch the visibility of **Walls or Furniture**.

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/yuntai/image/png/1779695251832/C709A4E4F6A47B36A86D49088F73D677.png)

2. To allow users to observe the interior space more conveniently, the system sets walls to be in the hide state by default. When you need to view the enclosed space effects or perform wall-related operations, check the **Walls** option to switch to the visible state.

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/image/png/1779692647633/A8CEBABD0663ECBAEEE7516DF7A7D2B5.png)

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/yuntai/image/gif/1779695251975/3A999E1633012EFB1124D481D8A046E9.gif)

3. **Furniture** is visible by default in the scene. When you need to adjust hard furnishings or plan the overall space, uncheck the option to avoid obstructions.

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/image/png/1779692695303/A8CEBABD0663ECBAEEE7516DF7A7D2B5.png)

![](https://qhstaticssl.kujiale.com/yuntai/image/gif/1779695251803/A183343947C3C81E67B922373D8CA2AB.gif)
