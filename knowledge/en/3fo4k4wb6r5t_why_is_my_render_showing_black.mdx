#### 

#### 1.Camera Positioning Issues

One of the most frequent causes of a black render is that the camera may be stuck inside a wall, closet, or another solid object within your design. When the camera is positioned incorrectly, it can limit visibility and block light from entering the scene, resulting in a completely dark render.

![](https://qhstatic-us-s3.coohom.com/yuntai/image/png/1742392622288/DE521EF1FFFE86FE9B0797A71FC74BD2.png)

### How to Fix It:

* **Check Camera Placement**: Navigate around your space to ensure the camera is not embedded in any objects.
* **Adjust Camera Angle**: If you find it stuck, reposition the camera to an open area where it has a clear line of sight.

#### 2. Insufficient Light Sources

Another reason for a black render could be inadequate lighting within your scene. If there aren’t enough light sources or if they are too dim, the rendering engine may struggle to produce a well-lit image.

### How to Fix It:

* **Add More Lights**: Introduce additional light sources such as lamps or ceiling fixtures to brighten up your space.
* **Adjust Light Settings**: Check and increase the intensity of existing lights if necessary to ensure adequate illumination throughout your design.

#### Conclusion

By addressing these two common issues—camera positioning and lighting—you can quickly resolve black renders in Coohom and continue bringing your creative visions to life. Always remember to double-check these elements before finalizing your designs!
